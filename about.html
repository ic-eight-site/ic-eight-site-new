About

Why IC Eight exists

IC Eight is built around one narrow problem: how English-first technology products become clear, credible, and usable in Japanese.

For much of my career, I worked on Japanese market-facing language for global technology companies: premium technology marketing, launch messaging, product copy, and product-adjacent content for brands including Apple, Meta, Microsoft, Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk, and CircleCI.

That work was never only about wording.

The hard part was knowing what Japanese users needed to understand, what the product needed them to trust, and what the English source copy assumed but never said.

Over time, the pattern became clear: many Japanese product problems are not caused by poor translation. They come from trying to build the Japanese experience from English copy after the product logic has already been set.

IC Eight was created for SaaS teams entering Japan that need a different starting point.

What this means in practice

We build Japanese product experiences from intent: what the user needs to understand, what the product needs them to do, and what the experience needs to make feel clear, trustworthy, and usable in Japanese.

The work is AI-native, but not automated away.

AI is used to generate, compare, structure, and scale. Human judgment stays where it matters: intent, tone, trust, usability, and whether the Japanese experience works as part of the product.

One operator. One language pair. No handoffs.

Native Japanese judgment. Fluent English communication.
